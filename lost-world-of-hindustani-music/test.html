About Hindustani Classical Music

Hindustani classical music is one of the oldest forms of music in the world, with roots in the ancient religious Vedic hymns, tribal chants, devotional temple music, and folk music.

Dhrupad Tradition

Dhrupad is the oldest surviving form of Indian Classical music, originating from the chanting of Vedic hymns. The nature of Dhrupad music is spiritual, seeking to induce feelings of peace and contemplation.

Kheyal Style

Kheyal is a form of vocal music whose name comes from the Arabic word "khayal" meaning imagination. This style allows more improvisation and ornamentation than Dhrupad.

Instrumental Music

Indian classical instrumental music has developed extensively over centuries. Instruments like sitar, sarod, tabla, shehnai, and flute have been used to create deeply spiritual and meditative music.
