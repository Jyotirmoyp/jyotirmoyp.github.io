Life in Franconia

I came to Bayreuth when the autum leaves were falling. The streets were colored with the red and yellow pigment of nature. I came to Bayreuth when every leaf was a flower. The winter was yet to arrive, nevertheless, my warm skin from the land of sun and shadow was already striving for warmth.









Just beside my room, a beautiful Franconian hotel veiled behind the autumn tree.









These are modern houses, not typical of Franconian. They look colourful along Richard Wagner Strasse.









There are many parks and gardens in the city. One day, I strolled around the famous garden, Ermitage. Every leaf was a flower.









Just out of the town, there is another castle. Fresh snow has just poured in the outskirts. This is a typical Franconian landscape.









There was a palace of fantasy. A silent lake, and a snow covered lone home for no one.









Snow gradually came to the city. It was 1st December, when snow started falling.









As December came, so came the light, the Christmas light. Markets were closed in the pandemic, only lights had the existence.









Then Christmas came. The city was flooded with snow. The city centre was empty, lonely as ever on the 25th December.









This is the iconic dinosaur at the city centre.









I was wondering the city during the deep snow. The water fountain was dead, frozen.









A small river flows behind my house, along the heart of the city. I walked along the bank of the river Main.









The river then lost in the jungle. Last few houses of the city remains.









Human, river, house, all are gone. Only an eternal snow field exists.









And there the city Franconian homeland, over the horizon, where the lone lady walks.









On 31st December, I came to Bamberg. This hanging Rathaus is the most iconic building of the city.









I have never seen such gigantic cathedral, this is Bamberg Catherdral, largest in Bavaria.









Typical Franconian households are drenched with morning sunlight.









Streets are also empty here, very close to Bamberg city centre on 31st December.









They were singing and wishing a Happy New Year.









January came. Snow was becoming thick. I went to Fitchtelbirge, a jungle near the city. Snow was knee deep.









Snow flows with strong wind. The pattern is distinct. The zebra of snow and bark.









I like this as a Japanese painting.









They were my company during the hike.









It was a lake. Now there is no trace of water. Will visit the same place once again in the autum.









One day snow melted. Sun came for few hours. I opened my balcony after several months. And the view was worth.









And finally I saw people on the street. They were happy. Singing, running.









The labyrinth of medieval city, there were people. Warmth was coming back.









Then I came to the largest city of Franconia. Nuremberg. The famous Nuremberg!









This is the famous city centre square. Once raised Jewish colony was obliterated during black plague.









A couple was soaking the sunlight inside this imperial castle, against the typical Franconian wall.









Local market of European cities. Typically with this red and white strips of cloth shades.









Like Paris, Nuremberg also have a couple of river islands. Much smaller in size, much cozy too.









One day, the day light saving hour has been stopped. 7 30 pm in the clock and the sun was still there.









Yet, the winter continues in the Fitchtelbirge region. For the first time, I met Alpacas.









Any top point of Fitchelbirge ridge has similar views. Dense Franconian forest and snow field beyond that.









During Easter, I went for first holiday. To Alps. From Munich I was going towards Innsbruck. This is lake Kochelsee.









We came to Cortini, a small village near Italian dolomites. And this was the view from my balcony.









Next few days we were hiking and driving along the folds of mountains. The hamlets were sparkling in the mountain sun.









Came to a frozen lake, Lago de Braise. We hiked upto the mountain, along the glacier and moraines.









A postcard picture village came on our way. The picture perfect European alps.









From Italy, I came to Switzerland. It was peak summer. The typical mustard fields were at full bloom.









That day was bit hazy. The Jungefrau were fading behind the tiny huts of Kline Schidogg.









Praha was another in the bucket list. So, I spent a few evenings walking on the bank of Valtava.









The final summer holiday was in Croatia. Though I have no idea about game of thrones, fans of that series can easily recognize this place.









Typical Mediterranean architecture are parts of Croat cities. This Zadar. The heart of Croat sea.









For a brief period of time, I was roaming around Budapest. A few evenings were not enough.









Probably the most iconic building in Hungary. Majestic!









This kind of supermarkets are common in eastern European city. I saw similar in Bratislava.

Came for a short visit to the country of my desire! Norway. And this iconic opera house is probably the most photographed building in Oslo.









Was strolling around the medieval fortress of Oslo. It was almost 9, and the sun was about to set. Some lone lady was still soaking the sunrays! Winter is coming again.









After long tours, came back to home. To Franconia. The last phase of Autumn was still there.









Often I take a stroll along these lonely paths into the jungle of Franconia.









If you remember the frozen lake house, an almost black and white picture during the winter, is the most colourful place during the fall.









This was an eternal snow field a few months back and it will be like that after a few months.









Another postcard size Franken house. Very close to my home.









The first snow appeared in early November. It was just a glimpse.









Again, back to that same house, where the river is lost in jungles. A scene from pre winter.









After two years, Christmas market was open. Nuremberg, famous for the occasion was a must visit.









I have never seen these much of crowd in any other German city!









Again winter came, an year has been passed. Lonely corners of the city are again frozen.









As the winter engulfs us, we become more accepting. Start enjoying the absence of the sun over a snow field to gaze towards the Franconian city.
