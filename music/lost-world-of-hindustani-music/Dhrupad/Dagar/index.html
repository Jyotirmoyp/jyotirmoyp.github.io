Dhrupad Tradition

Dhrupad is the oldest surviving form of Indian Classical music, originating from the chanting of Vedic hymns.

The nature of Dhrupad music is spiritual, seeking to induce feelings of peace and contemplation rather than merely to entertain.

History of Dhrupad

Dhrupad evolved from the earlier chanting style known as Prabandha, with its present form crystallizing in the 15th and 16th centuries during the reign of Emperor Akbar.

Characteristics

Dhrupad is characterized by its solemnity, emphasis on lyrical content, and minimal use of ornamentation compared to other forms like Khyal.

Notable Exponents

The Dagar family has been instrumental in preserving and promoting the Dhrupad tradition for generations.
